About Me

Hi, I'm Vaibhav. I'm a skilled and experienced professional with wide ranging exposure across industries and verticals. I have worked as a Software Engineer in Telecom, Order, Revenue and Billing Management domains, as a Corporate Finance/Accounting professional in the Insurance domain; and now applying that experience in FP&A/Management Consulting space as a Data Scientist and EPM (Anaplan) professional.

In my current role, I am working as a Data Scientist where I perform tasks such as monthly model runs for active/winback/whitespace/lost clients (using Random Forest), using Logistic Regression to map use-cases to clients and being involved with a Natural Language Processing (NLP) model to match clients to use-cases using semantic information extraction and word embedding vectors (BERT, Spacy, word2vec etc).

Knowledge in NLP modeling and fine-tuning BERT and similar large language models using Tensorflow and PyTorch. Experienced in making data visualization and dashboard using Tableau, D3.js, Javascript and jQuery.

I'm also deeply involved in the annual finance planning & budgeting cycle by performing various EPM tasks using my expertise with Anaplan.

Data Science Capstone

MakeSense - Twitch listener and sentiment analysis

Twitch is a live streaming service and global community for content spanning gaming, entertainment, music, sports, and more. As of August 2022, it had 9.2 million monthly streamers, 140 million active monthly users. Twitch's top streamers earned over $30,000 a month from their streaming and content channels on the site.

Our MIDS capstone, product attempted to provide additional insights to Twitch’s analytics by interpreting chat behavior and providing sentiment scores of chat messages during a stream.

DATA + PEOPLE = POWER

Communication Unbreakdown, our Data Science capstone team, has paired grassroots organizing with cutting-edge data science to help community organizers reach target populations for civic engagement. Our solution helped People’s Action reach 33% more conflicted voters in the last two weeks of the U.S. presidential election in five key swing states where the technology was used.

The technology was also used for the Georgia Senate runoff. During the final days of voting, conflicted voters were found in 45% of people contacted compared to a human-informed control group of 18%.
